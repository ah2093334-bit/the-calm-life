Vitamins and Minerals: What Your Body Actually Needs

⚠️ This article is for general education only and is not medical advice. Supplement use should be discussed with a doctor.

Vitamins and minerals do not provide energy directly, but they are essential for nearly every process in your body, from healing wounds to keeping your heart beating steadily.

Key vitamins worth knowing

Vitamin D supports bone health and immune function, but many people are low on it since it mainly comes from sunlight, not food. Vitamin B12 is essential for nerve function and red blood cells, and is only found naturally in animal products. Vitamin C supports immunity and skin healing, and is easily obtained from fruits and vegetables.

Key minerals worth knowing

Iron carries oxygen in your blood; low levels cause fatigue and are common, especially in women. Calcium supports bone strength throughout life, not just in childhood. Magnesium is involved in hundreds of body processes, yet is commonly under-consumed.

Why deficiencies often go unnoticed

Mild deficiencies frequently cause vague symptoms like fatigue or low mood, which are easy to attribute to stress instead. This is why unexplained, persistent fatigue is worth checking with a simple blood test.

✅ Good natural sources

  • Leafy greens (iron, calcium)
  • Fatty fish (vitamin D, B12)
  • Citrus fruits (vitamin C)
  • Nuts and seeds (magnesium)
  • Eggs and dairy (B12, calcium)

⚠️ Common gaps

  • Vitamin D (limited sun exposure)
  • B12 (plant-based diets without supplementation)
  • Iron (especially in menstruating women)
  • Magnesium (highly processed diets)

A grounded approach

Most healthy adults get what they need from a varied diet. Supplements are useful for specific, identified gaps, best guided by an actual blood test rather than guesswork.
