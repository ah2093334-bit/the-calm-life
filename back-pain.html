Why Your Back Hurts (And What Actually Helps)

⚠️ This article is for general education only and is not medical advice. For severe, persistent, or radiating back pain, please see a doctor.

Back pain is one of the most common reasons people see a doctor worldwide — and the good news is that most cases aren't caused by anything serious, but by everyday posture and movement patterns that quietly add up.

The most common cause: not a single injury

Most back pain isn't caused by one dramatic event — it builds gradually from prolonged sitting, weak core muscles, poor posture, and repetitive movement patterns that place uneven stress on the spine over time.

Why sitting is so hard on your back

Sitting puts significantly more pressure on spinal discs than standing does — especially when slouched forward, which is the default posture for most desk and phone use. Combined with weak core muscles (which normally help support the spine), this is the single biggest contributor to modern back pain.

Other common contributors

Weak core muscles mean the spine bears more load than it should during everyday movement. Poor sleep position, particularly stomach sleeping, can strain the lower back overnight. Lifting incorrectly (bending from the back instead of the knees) puts sudden, uneven stress on the spine.

Habits that genuinely help

✅ Helps your back

  • Standing or moving every 30-60 minutes
  • Core-strengthening exercise
  • Lifting with your knees, not your back
  • Sleeping on your side or back
  • Keeping screens at eye level

⚠️ Makes it worse

  • Prolonged slouched sitting
  • Weak, untrained core muscles
  • Lifting heavy objects by bending forward
  • Stomach sleeping
  • Ignoring early, mild discomfort

When back pain needs medical attention

Pain that radiates down a leg, causes numbness, follows an injury, or doesn't improve after a couple of weeks should be evaluated by a doctor rather than managed alone.

For most people, a stronger core and less prolonged sitting solve more back pain than any mattress or gadget claims to.
