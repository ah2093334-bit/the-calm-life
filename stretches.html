Simple Stretches for People Who Sit All Day

Sitting for eight hours a day puts constant strain on the same few muscles — your hips, lower back, and shoulders. You don't need a gym or extra time to fix this. Five minutes, done consistently, is enough.

1. Neck rolls

Slowly drop your ear toward one shoulder, roll your chin to your chest, then to the other shoulder. Repeat slowly for 30 seconds. This releases tension that builds up from looking at a screen.

2. Seated spinal twist

Sitting upright, place one hand on the back of your chair and gently twist your torso toward it. Hold for 15 seconds, then switch sides. This counters the forward-hunched position most desks encourage.

3. Hip flexor stretch

Stand up, step one foot back, and gently push your hips forward until you feel a stretch at the front of your hip. Sitting all day shortens this muscle, which is often the real cause of lower back pain.

4. Shoulder blade squeeze

Pull your shoulder blades back and together, as if trying to hold a pencil between them. Hold for 5 seconds, release, repeat 10 times. This directly counters the rounded-shoulder posture from typing.

5. Standing forward fold

Stand up, let your arms hang, and slowly bend forward from your hips, letting your head relax down. This releases the entire back of your body in one simple move.

Set a reminder every couple of hours to do one of these. Small, frequent movement beats one long stretch session at the end of the day.
