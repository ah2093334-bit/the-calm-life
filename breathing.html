A 2-Minute Breathing Exercise for Anxious Moments

When anxiety spikes, your breathing usually gets short and shallow without you noticing — which keeps your body in an alert state. This simple pattern, called box breathing, gives your nervous system a clear signal to calm down.

The pattern

Breathe in slowly for 4 seconds. Hold for 4 seconds. Breathe out slowly for 4 seconds. Hold again for 4 seconds. That's one round.

How to do it

Sit or stand comfortably. Repeat the pattern for 4 to 6 rounds — about two minutes. It helps to trace a square shape with your eyes or a finger as you go, one side per step.

Why it works

Slow, controlled breathing activates your parasympathetic nervous system — the part of your body responsible for calming you down. It's used by athletes, and even by first responders, specifically because it works fast and requires nothing but attention.

You can do this anywhere — before a meeting, in a waiting room, or lying in bed. No app, no equipment, no one needs to know you're doing it.
